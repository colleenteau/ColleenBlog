If you understand the principles of neuroplasticity and that it’s the brains ability to continuously change and adapt throughout your life, this will be hugely beneficial to your learning. Once a person realises that their brain is capable of taking on any new knowledge and skills it creates a positive environment to learn. The best way to increase neuroplasticity is to practice and engage regularly with what you are wanting to learn. The more effort the bigger the reward. I am finding that this is the case on my own learning journey and even if I’m not understanding the information so long as I just keep on trying and listening and being open to the idea it will be come easier. It does. Slowly, but it happens.

Growth mindset can be the difference between success and failure so the relevance of growth mindset is also greatly important. Although I have been aware of this concept for a long time, I realised when I hit a difficult part of my learning process in sprint 2, that I hadn’t been practicing in a growth mindset. After many years of working in an area where I was pretty confident and knowledgeable, I totally struggled when faced with brand new information which was totally unlike the environment and teachings I have had to date. I was quickly sinking into a ‘fixed’ mindset and questioning my intelligence, abilities, my parenting, my relationship, myself as a person. This week I still have the same struggles with understanding the concepts but I have a renewed sense of faith in myself and trust in the process. Thanks to tapping back into my long since used ‘growth’ mindset.
